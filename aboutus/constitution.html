  1. OBJECTIVES:

    The New Brunswick Chinese Cultural Association is a non-profit organization having no affiliation with any religion or political party. The objectives of the Association are:

    1. to conduct classes teaching Chinese language and culture
    2. to help permeate Chinese culture and promote multi-culturalism in Canada;
    3. to foster friendship and cooperation among Canadians of Chinese ethnic origin in the province of New Brunswick; and
    4. to promote understanding and goodwill between Chinese-Canadians and other Canadian citizens in New Brunswick. (back to top)
  2. MEMBERSHIP:
    1. Any person who is a legal resident of New Brunswick is eligible to become member of the Association.
    2. There are two categories of membership: Adult (age 18 and above) and junior (under the age of 18)
    3. Members are obliged to observe the Constitution of the Association and pay membership dues.
    4. Members and their families have the privilege to participate in all activities of the Association.
    5. Adult members are eligible to be elected to the Board of Directors, and as officers of the Executive Council, and to vote at the general Assembly.
    6. Members are obliged to assist in all functions undertaken by the Association.(back to top)
  3. THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY:
    1. The General Assembly shall meet at least once in each calendar year. A quorum of the meeting shall be attendance of at least twenty of adult members of the Association.
    2. The president of the Association may call a Special Assembly at the request of the Executive Council.
    3. The General Assembly shall exercise the following powers:
      1. To discuss and approve policies of the Association, and entrust the Executive Council with their administration;
      2. To amend the Constitution by a two-third majority vote of adult members attending the Assembly;
      3. To elect the President of the Association and other officers of the Executive Council;
      4. To approve the work plan proposed by the Executive Council; and
      5. To approve new membership applications and annual membership dues proposed by the Executive Council.
    4. The General Assembly shall be held at such place and on such date as the Executive Council determines. (back to top)
  4. BOARD OF DIRECTORS:

    The affairs of the Association shall be managed by a Board of eight Directors who shall be elected members of the Executives Council of the Association. (back to top)

  5. THE EXECUTIVE COUNCIL:
    1. The officers of the Executive Council shall be elected for a term of one year from the date of election or until the first General Assembly after such date.
    2. The Executive Council shall administer the policies and organize, manage, and direct the activities authorized by the Assembly, and may exercise all such powers and do everything that may be exercised or done by the Association and are not by the Constitution or bylaws of the Association or by law expressly directed or required to be done by the Association at a General or Special Assembly.
    3. The Executive Council shall consist of the following officers:
      1. A President who shall be the chief executive officer of the Association and shall exercise general supervision over the activities of the Executive Council and the business and affairs of the Association. The President shall, when present, preside at all meetings of the assembly and the Executive Council; he/she shall be the official representative of the Association in external relations; and shall posses and may exercise such powers assigned to him/her by the Assembly;
      2. A Vice-president who shall assume the duties and powers of the President during the latter's absence or inability to act. The Vice-president shall be responsible for special projects initiated by the Association.
      3. A Chairperson of the Education Committee who shall also be known as the dean of the faculty. The dean shall have charge of all matters pertaining to the educational and academic activities organized and conducted by the Association, and without limiting the generality of the foregoing, he/she shall be responsible for the following:
        1. Arranging curricula, recommending teachers to the Council, controlling admission, maintaining student records, and supervising all other activities relating to academic affairs;
        2. Overseeing the safety of students while in the school; and
        3. Assisting in and promoting the development of academic personnel.
      4. A Vice-chairperson of the Education Committee who shall assist the Chairperson in all capacities and who shall be responsible mainly for directing extra-curricula activities.
      5. A Secretary who shall be responsible for:
        1. Keeping an accurate record of the proceedings of all meeting of the Assembly and the Council;
        2. Conducting the correspondence of the Association under the direction of the Executive; and
        3. Giving all notices required to be given to members and executives officers and keeping safe custody of all books, papers, records, correspondences, contracts, and other documents belonging to the Association.
      6. A Public Relation Officer in charge of publicity and internal communications.
      7. A Managing Officer in charge of purchasing and property management, and shall perform such other duties as are from time to time assigned by the Council.
      8. A Treasurer who shall be responsible for collecting membership dues and tuition as approved by the Council, and shall keep full and accurate accounts of all receipts and disbursements of the Association in such banks as may be from time to time designated by the Council. He/she has charge of all the finances of the Association and shall present an annual financial report to the General Assembly.
    4. In the event of a vacancy occurring in any office for any cause, the Executive Council may appoint a member of the Association to fill the vacancy for the remainder of the term. (back to top)
  6. AMENDMENT:

    This Constitution may be amended, altered, or replaced by a two-third majority vote of adult members at any General or Special Assembly, provided notice of intention to amend the said Constitution shall be given one week in advance of the meeting. (back to top)
